Real Estate (Part 2)

Inflation Protection

  • Rental payments tend to increase with inflation
  • Investors reposition their portfolios toward real assets during inflationary periods

Introduction to NCREIF and the NCREIF Indexes

National Council of Real Estate Investment Fiduciaries (NCREIF)

Objectives:

  • Collect data
  • Publish indices
  • Promote good standards
  • Support independent research
  • Conduct and publish research

NCREIF Property Index (NPI)

  • Created to measure the performance of income-producing real properties
  • Assumes properties are unleveraged
  • Relies on appraisals - leads to smoothing (lower volatility)
  • Components (Largest-to-Smallest): Office, Apartment, Retail, Industrial, Hotels
  • Returns are calculuated quarterly based on quarterly appraisals

Effects of Smoothing

  1. Lagging values
  2. Low volatility
  3. Low correlation

Unsmoothing Methods

  1. Use current transactions
  2. Hedonic price index - uses actual transactions that attributes property value changes to property charactersistics
  3. Use only revalued properties
  4. Econometric adjustment - use current appraisals, previous appraisal, and a decay parameter

National Association of Real Estate Investment Trusts (NAREIT) index

  • Composed of publicly-traded REITs

Real Estate as an Investment

Real Estate Investment Goals

  • Ample cash flows
  • Returns in excess of risk-free rate
  • Inflation hedge
  • Better reflect the global investment opportunity set
  • Provide diversification
Note:

Note: Direct investments in real estate (NPI indices and subindices) achieve all 5 goals. Indirect investments (NAREIT index) achieve 4 goals, but do not provide inflation hedging.
