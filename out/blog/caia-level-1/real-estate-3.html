Results
  • NPI - Index and its subindices have strong diversification potential when combined with traditional assets (stock and bonds)
  • Traditional asset classes do not provide inflation protection (low correlation with inflation)
  • REITs do not provide inflation protection (low correlation with inflation)
  • Direct real estate (NPI indices) provides the greatest improvement in the efficient frontier. (shift curve "up and to the left")
  • REITs provide improvement in the efficient frontier (shift curve "up" only)
  • REITs tend to diversify bond portfolios very well and diversify large stock portfolios better than small stock portfolios.

Core, Value-Added, and Opportunistic Real Estate

Reasons for Classifying Real Estate
  • Obtain style diversification
  • Monitor style drift
  • Measure performance
Real Estate Investment Styles

  • Core: large percentage of returns from cash flow, low volatility

    • Retail, offices, apartment, industrial
    • Low leverage
    • High occupancy
    • Full operating (i.e. leased)
    • Returns should fall within 25th and 75th percentiles (interquartile range)
    • Expected return: 9%-10% (absolute), NPI return (relative)
    • Volatility: 11%

  • Value-Added: significant percentage of returns from price appreciation, moderate volatility

    • retail, offices, apartments, industrial, storage, healthcare, hospitality, low-income
    • Moderate leverage
    • Moderate to well-leased, pre-leased development
    • Operating and leasing
    • Returns should fall within 5th and 25th percentiles initially, then within 75th and 95th percentiles
    • Required return: 10%-13% (absolute), NPI + 200 bps (relative)
    • Volatility: 10%-13%

  • Opportunistic: returns primarily from price appreciation, high volatility due to leverage, development risk, leasing risk, or a combination of moderate risk factors

    • Nontraditional properties, speculative development, raw land
    • High leverage
    • Low occupancy
    • New construction and development
    • Returns should fall below 5th percentile initially, then above 95th percentile
    • Required return: ≥13% (absolute), ≥NPI + 500 bps (relative)
    • Volatility: ≥13%

Private Equity Real Estate (PERE)

  • Funds used to gain exposure to opportunistic real estate, especially when located in a foreign nation

  • Major Risks

    • Zoning and public policy risk
    • Development risk
    • Currency risk
    • Tenant risk
    • Property turnaround risk

  • For real estate, the relative return is expressed relative to the NPI (NCREIF Property Index).

  • Many real estate investments made in foreign countries are taxable in the domestic country.
