Covariance and Correlation

Covariance

  • A nonstandardized measure of the linear relationship between two variables that ranges from negative infinity to positive infinity
  • Positive (+) covariance means the variables tend to move in the same direction and vice versa
  • Calculated as the expected value of the product of the deviations of each variable from their respective means
  • Units are squared units of the variables being measured and are not standardized so are less meaningful

Correlation

  • A standardized measure of the linear relationship between two variables that ranges from -1 to +1
  • Positive (+) correlation means the variables tend to move in the same direction and vice versa
  • Calculated as the covariance of the two variables divided by the product of their standard deviations

Significance of Correlation Coefficient

  • Null Hypothesis: ρ = 0 (no linear relationship between X and Y)
  • Alternative Hypothesis: p not equal to 0 (there is a non-zero correlation with X% confidence level)
  • Two-tailed test
  • Degrees of freedom = n - 2

Limitations to Correlation Analysis

  • Outliers distort the correlations
  • Spurious correlation (linear relationship without economic rationale)
  • Nonlinear relationships cannot be captured with correlation
