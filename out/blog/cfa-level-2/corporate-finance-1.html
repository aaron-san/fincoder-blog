Capital Budgeting

Principles

  1. Use cash flows
    • Incremental cash flows
    • Include externality cannibalization effects
    • Adjust for changes in working capital
  2. Use cash flows based on opportunity costs
  3. Timing is important (time value of money)
  4. Adjust for tax effects
  5. Financing costs are accounted for in required return

Purposes

  1. Expansion - investment for business growth
  2. Replacement - investment to replace existing assets

Depreciation

  • Accelerated methods (e.g., MACRS) provide higher tax savings in earlier years compared to straight-line

Projects with Unequal Lives

  • Least common multiple of lives approach (look at NPVs over a common life)
  • Equivalent Annual Annuity (EAA) (find the annuity (PMT) that equates the NPVs at the cost of capital)

Discounting Cash Flows

  • Discount nominal cash flows at nominal rate, or discount real cash flows at real rate
  • Unexpected changes in inflation affect profitability
  • Inflation affects revenues and expenses differently
  • Unexpectedly high inflation affects the value of fixed payments received (e.g., by bondholders)

Operating Cash Flows

T = Marginal tax rate
Dep = Depreciation expense

where:
Cash OpEx = Cash operating expenses
T = Marginal tax rate
Dep = Depreciation expense

2. Replacement Projects

Initial Cash Outflow
Initial cash outflow = FC INV + ΔWC - Proceeds BT + T×(Proceeds BT - BV)\mathrm {\small { \text {Initial cash outflow = FC$_{\text { INV}}$ + $\Delta$WC - Proceeds$_{\text { BT}}$ + T$\times$(Proceeds$_{\text { BT}}$ - BV)}}}Initial cash outflow = FC INV​ + ΔWC - Proceeds BT​ + T×(Proceeds BT​ - BV)
where:

FC INV = Fixed capital investment in new asset

Proceeds BT = Pre-tax sale proceeds from sale of existing fixed capital asset

ΔWC = Change in net working capital investment (i.e., Net working capital investment)

T = Marginal tax rate
BV = Book value of existing asset
Operating Cash Flows
Operating cash flows = (ΔSales - ΔCash OpEx)(1 - T) + ΔDep×T\mathrm {\small { \text {Operating cash flows = ($\Delta$Sales - $\Delta$Cash OpEx)(1 - T) + $\Delta$Dep$\times$T}}}Operating cash flows = (ΔSales - ΔCash OpEx)(1 - T) + ΔDep×T
where:
ΔSales = Incremental increase in sales from new machine

ΔCash OpEx = Incremental change in cash operating expenses from new machine

ΔDep = Change in depreciation expense
T = Marginal tax rate
Terminal Cash Flow
Terminal year non-operating cash flow = ΔProceedsBT + ΔWC - T × (ΔProceedsBT - ΔBV)\mathrm {\small { \text {Terminal year non-operating cash flow = $\Delta$Proceeds$_{\text {BT}}$ + $\Delta$WC - T $\times$ ($\Delta$Proceeds$_{\text {BT}}$ - $\Delta$BV)}}}Terminal year non-operating cash flow = ΔProceedsBT​ + ΔWC - T × (ΔProceedsBT​ - ΔBV)
where:

ΔProceeds BT = Difference in pre-tax sale proceeds from sale of fixed capital asset

ΔWC = Change in net working capital investment (i.e., Net working capital investment)

T = Marginal tax rate
ΔBV = Difference in book value of asset sold
