Recaptured Depreciation

  • Recaptured depreciation represents depreciation that was taken in anticipation of a decline in the value of an asset that ultimately did not materialize.
  • Recaptured depreciation = Accumulated depreciation (A/D)
  • Tax on recaptured depreciation = Accumulated depreciaiton _ tax on recaptured depreciation Tax on capital gains = tax rate on capital gains _ (capital gain - recaptured depreciation)

Private Equity Valuation

Definitions Portfolio Company - companies that private equity firms invest in Private Equity Firm - companies that create portfolios of portfolio companies and allow PE investors to invest in Private Equity Investor - outside investors who makes an investment in a fund offered by the PE firm

Unique Sources of Value for Private Equity Investments

  1. Ability to re-engineer the firm for efficiency
  2. Ability to acquire debt at favorable terms
  3. Superior alignment of interests between managers and owners

Definitions

  • Compensation - payment that is closely linked to the firm's performance
  • Tag-Along, Drag-Along Clauses - the requirement that an acquiring firm extend the acquisition offer to all shareholders, including firm management
  • Board Representation - The private equity firm is ensured control through board representation in a major event Noncompete Clauses - Company founders must sign a clause that prevents them from competing against the firm withina prespecified period of time

Priority in Claims

  • Private equity firms receive their distributions before other owners

Required Approvals

  • Changes of strategic importance must be approved by the private equity firm

Earn-Outs

  • These tie the acquisition price paid by the private equity firm to the portfolio company's future performance over a specified time period.
