### Accrual vs. Cash Accounting

#### Cash Accounting

- Transaction recorded upon receipt of cash
- No subjectivity and easy to verify

#### Accrual Accounting

- Revenue and expenses triggered by earnings process
- **Advantage**: Timely and relevant information with indication of value creating activities
- **Disadvantage**: Subjective measurement and earnings management

### Earnings
- Quality = persistence and sustainability
- Mean reversion
- Cash flow and accruals elements
- **Advantage**: Accruals element is not sustainable (Accruals naturally self correct)
- **Disadvantage**: Cash earnings don't always reflect earned revenues and can be used to manipulate earnings

### Earnings Management

#### Revenue Recognition Problems

##### Range of problems

  - Recognition of sale before completion of earnings process
  - Recognition of sale without assurance of receipt

##### Estimates
  - Credit sales
  - Deferred/Unearned revenue
  - Warranty provisions
  - Sales returns

##### Warning Signs
- Large increase in accounts receivable
- Large drop in unearned revenue
- Lower future cash flows and accounting rates of return

#### Accelerating Revenue Recognition

##### Range of problems:
  - Recognition of sale before completion of earnings process
  - Lowering credit standards
  - Cut-off issues (moving the sales between periods)

##### Warning Signs
- Bundled products
- Management vested options ITM
- Pressure to meet earnings forecasts
- Raising additional finance
- Large rise in accounts receivables
- Large drop in unearned revenue
- Dispraportionate revenue in last quarter

#### Recognizing Revenue Too Early
- Bill-and-hold sales
- Lessor use of sales type vs. direct financing leases
- Recording sales prior to acceptance by customer (e.g., sales of equipment prior to installation)
- Incorrectly using Percent-Completion Method for long-term contracts
