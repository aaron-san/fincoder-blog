## Economics

### Capital Market expectations

- Benefits:
  1.  Helps decide stategic asset allocation (SAA)
  2.  Helps detect short-term mispricings through tactical asset allocation (TAA)
  3.  Used in equity/bond valuation (beta research)

#### Process

- Assess drivers of past performance
- Choose valuation model
- Evaluate data to be used
- Formulate expectations using Black-Litterman, DCF, etc.
- Monitor performance and refine process

#### Weaknesses

- Data measurement errors and bias
- Use of ex-post data
- Non-repeating patterns
- Failure to incorporate conditioning information
- Misinterpretation of correlations
- Psychological traps
- Model and input uncertainty

- The variance of market i, given a covariance of factor returns:

<Math>
  {
    "$\\sigma_{i}^2 = \\beta_{i,1}^2\\sigma_{F,1}^2 + \\beta_{i,2}^2\\sigma_{F,2}^2 + 2\\beta_{i,1}\\beta_{i,2}Cov(F_{1},F_{2}) + \\sigma_{\\varepsilon,i}^2$"
  }
</Math>

- The covariance between two markets i and j:

<Math>
  {
    "$Cov(i,j) = \\beta_{i,1}\\beta_{j,1}\\sigma_{F,1}^2 + \\beta_{i,2}\\beta_{j,2}\\sigma_{F,2}^2 + (\\beta_{i,1}\\beta_{j,2} + \\beta_{i,2}\\beta_{j,1})Cov(F_{1},F_{2})$"
  }
</Math>

### Economic Growth

#### Growth Trends (long-term)

1. Changes in employment levels

   - Population growth
   - Labor Rate Participation Rate

2. Changes in productivity

   - Spending on new capital inputs
   - Total factor productivity growth

#### Cyclical (short-term)

1. Inventory cycle
  - Fluctuations in the stock of goods a company holds driven by demand and production
  - Can influence the business cycle
2. Business cycle
  - Overall ecenomic expansions and contractions that are often magnified by inventory cycle effects
  - Encompasses total output, GDP, empoyment, etc.
  - Driven by aggregate demand and supply, technological advancements and government policy

### Relative Equity Market Valuation

#### Earnings-based

1. The Fed Model
2. The Yardeni Model

##### The Fed Model

<Math>
  {
    "$Ratio = \\frac {\\text {\\normalsize {S\\&P Current Earnings Yield}}}{\\text {\\normalsize {Long-term T-bond yield}}} = \\frac {\\text {\\normalsize {CEY}}}{\\text {\\normalsize {TBY}}}$"
  }
</Math>

<div className="math-var-keys" style={{marginLeft: "50px"}}>
  <div>where:</div>
  <div>CEY = 1 / leading P/E</div>
</div>

<Math>
  {
    "$CEY = \\frac {\\text {\\normalsize {Aggregate 12-month forward earnings}}}{\\text {\\normalsize {Current S\\&P level}}} = \\frac {\\text {\\normalsize {E$_1$}}}{\\text {\\normalsize {P$_0$}}}$"
  }
</Math>

#####
- CEY should equal TBY, but if:
  - Ratio > 1, equities are undervalued and should rise
  - Ratio < 1, equities are overvalued and should fall

##### Problems with the Fed model

- Ignores the equity risk premium
