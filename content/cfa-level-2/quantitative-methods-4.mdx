## Time Series Analysis

### Time Series Models
1. Trend models (variable vs. time)
2. Autoregressive models (variable vs. earlier values of itself)
3. Autoregressive Conditional Heteroskedasticity (ARCH) models (applicable when the variance error term is depenedent on the size of earlier errors)

### Trend Models
1. Linear Trend Models
- Assumes average change in y is constant (linearly related to t by a magnitude of b (slope coefficient))
<Math>{"$y_t = b_0 + b_1t + \\epsilon_t$"}</Math>

#### Limitations of Trend Models
- It may be insufficient to model the dependent variable a single independent variable

### Log-Linear Trend Model
- The natrual log of the dependent variable is linearly modeled over time
- Useful when there is an exponential trend in underlying output variable versus time
- Assumes the average rate of change in the dependent variable is constant (e^(b1) - 1)
- Can help remove the problem of serially correlated errors in a simple linear trend model


<Math>{"$ln(y_t) = b_0 + b_1t + \\epsilon_t$"}</Math>
